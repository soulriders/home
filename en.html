Development Narrative

Flying Car: From Dream to Reality

Everyone dreams of a flying car at least once. We did too. We believed the tiltrotor would be the aircraft that could make that dream real. But in 1992, watching the crash of the Osprey V-22 left us with one question. If an aircraft is to fly above cities where people live, does it not need a much safer tiltrotor?

An Old Dream, Three Barriers

Take off and land in place. Fly far. Move safely between those two flight states. Until now, no aircraft had all three together.

The Possibility of the Lambda Engine

In 2006, looking at Korea's independently developed automotive engine Lambda, we asked whether that engine could become an aircraft engine. On paper, flying with automotive parts was already possible.

The Invention of the Asynchronous Tiltrotor

We drew and erased more than 200 forms of hover takeoff and landing aircraft. At the end of that process, we reached a structure where rotors tilt independently, one by one: the asynchronous tiltrotor. We filed the patent in 2020.

2026, Completion of the SDA Architecture

Starting from the first patent of 2020, we redesigned a patent network that connects the entire operating process from takeoff to emergency landing. The system completed in 2026 is the SDA architecture: five core technologies and about 25 patents.

1. It responds only as defined

The aircraft calculates the answers to defined situations in advance and switches instantly, so the outcome can be shown to certification authorities before flight.

2. It can be controlled and landed even when the engines stop

The eight rotors create differential drag and form a virtual control surface. Even without power, the aircraft direction and trajectory remain controlled.

Strategic Essay

The Essential Competitiveness of Lambda

A software-defined aircraft that removes engineering difficulty and leaves the utility of aviation.

Lambda structurally removes the reasons conventional aircraft become difficult.

Conventional aircraft add mechanical structures, control surfaces, links, hydraulic devices, variable-pitch systems, and complex control systems to gain performance and functions. Lambda was designed in the opposite direction.

It implements required flight functions through independent tilting of identical fixed-pitch propulsion units and software-defined flight modes.

Lambda transfers aircraft functions from complex mechanical devices to independent propulsion units and software-defined flight modes, lowering the difficulty and cost of manufacturing, maintenance, certification, and operation at the same time.
